A bit of history:

The history of the tic-tac-toe game dates back to ancient Egypt, where three-in-a-row board games were played around 1300 BC. In the Roman Empire, around the first century BC, a variation called terni lapilli emerged, limiting each player to three pieces.


The term "tick-tack-toe" was first printed in 1884, describing a children's game played on a slate. Another possible origin is "tick-tack," an old version of backgammon mentioned in 1558. In the 20th century, the US adopted the name "tic-tac-toe."


In 1952, OXO (Noughts and Crosses) was developed by British computer scientist Sandy Douglas for the EDSAC computer, marking one of the first video games. The computer player could play perfect tic-tac-toe against humans.


In 1975, MIT students demonstrated computational power using Tinkertoy elements for tic-tac-toe. The resulting Tinkertoy computer, mostly constructed from Tinkertoys, is now showcased at the Computer History Museum.
